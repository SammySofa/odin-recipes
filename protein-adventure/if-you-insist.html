Berry was born in a pasture. Its hills rolled like a green ocean beneath a peaceful sky. First, she had warmth... her mother's steady breath, the calming sun on her back, the quiet rhythm of a world that asked nothing of her but to live.

She learned quickly that life was not only warmth.

Fences surrounded her. Her eyes fixed on whatever lay beyond, but forever out of reach. She would stand at their edges sometimes, her dark eyes fixed on the horizon. She could feel it calling. The older cow, her mother included, never looked that way. They kept their heads low, chewing. Content or resigned, Berry could tell. She was not like them

Life, though, has a way of narrowing.

Seasons passed. Calves were born. Some stayed, some didn't. Berry learned the quiet grief that lived in absences. She would call for them at night sometimes, low and aching, until her voice blended into the hum of insects and the indifference of the stars.
