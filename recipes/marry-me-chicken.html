Marry Me Chicken Recipe

"Wow, what a scrumptious, creamy, easy dish!" -your future spouse

They say the way to someone's heart is through their stomach. This chicken is not the key, but it may be someday. Marry Me Chicken is basically a shortcut, though. With a sauce this creamy and sun-dried tomatoes this tangy, you might want to have a ring box hiding in the kitchen just in case. Sometimes I make it for myself.

Ingredients for cookin' somethin' up:

Protein:

  • 3 large, boneless/skinless hicken breast, sliced lengthwise into thin cutlets.

Aromatics & Fresh Herbs:

  • 3 cloves minced garlic
  • 1/3 cup chopped sundried tomatoes | packed in oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh basil leaves | for garnish

Spices & Dried Seasonings:

  • 1 tsp chili flakes | for a little kick
  • 1/4 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp salt | plus more for taste

The Jazz:

  • 1 cup heavy cream | also known as double cream
  • 1 cup chicken stock | vegetable stock is acceptable if you only have that
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 6 tablespoons all-purpose flour | for dredging the chicken
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil | for browning the chicken
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter | also for browning the chicken

Instructions:

  1. Season those chicken breats with salt and pepper, then dredge them in flour.
  2. Melt the butter into the oil over medium heat in a heavy-bottomed pan. If the butter starts browning too fast, turn the heat down... we want a romantic sizzle, not a kitchen fire.
  3. Tan the chicken in the pan. Make sure it's a nice and even. You want them looking like they just got back from a week in Mexico. Once they're golden and cooked through, move them to a warm plate and cover them up so they stay juicy.
  4. The Flavor Bomb... Toss in the minced garlic. You only have about 60 seconds before it turns bitter and ruins your life, so move fast! Pour in the chicken stock to deglaze the pan. Add the heavy cream and Parmesan. Let it simmer for a couple minutes.
  5. Throw in the chili flakes, herbs, and sundried tomatoes. Nestle the chicken back into the sauce like they're tucking into a cozy bed. Let it all simmer together until the sauce is thick enough to hold a secret.
  6. Garnish with fresh basil for a pop of color... presentation is 90% of the proposal. Serve this over pasta or rice, and prepare yourself for a trip to the diamond shop.
