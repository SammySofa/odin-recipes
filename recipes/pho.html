How to Make Pho

Pho is a soul-warming noodle soup.

This Vietnamese dish balances deep, savory complexity with vibrant freshness. Though many would point to the noodles as its primary ingredient, its heart is in the fragrant, spice-infused broth.

You can buy a quick & simple kit at the local grocery store just about anywhere, but traditionally, the broth is simmered for hours with charred ginger, onions, and star anise, and topped with fresh basil, lime, chili, and crunchy bean sprouts. Don't even get me started on the protein. I'll tell you all about the broth recipe here.

Ingredients you'll need:

Aromatics:

  • 2 large onions | yellow, halved
  • 150 g (5 oz) ginger | sliced down the center

Spices:

  • 10 star anise
  • 4 cinnamon quills
  • 4 cardamon pods
  • 3 (spice) cloves
  • 1.5 tbsp coriander seeds

Beef:

  • 1.5 kg (3 lb) beef brisket
  • 1 kg (2 lb) meaty beef bones
  • 1 kg (2 lb) marrow bones | leg, knuckle -- cut to reveal marrow

Seasoning:

  • 2 tbsp white sugar
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 40 mL (3 tbsp) fish sauce

Noodle Soup (per bowl):

  • 50 g (1.5 oz) dried rice sticks | or 120 g (4 oz) fresh
  • 30 g (1 oz) beef tenderloin, raw, very thinly sliced | partially freeze (about 30 minutes), then slice as thinly as possible. Pre thinly sliced frozen beef may also be available from Asian butchers.

Toppings (optional, but encouraged):

  • Beansprouts
  • Thai basil
  • Coriander/cilantro
  • Lime wedges (squeezed)
  • Finely slived red chilli
  • Hoisin sauce
  • Sriracha

Instructions:

1. Aromatics:

  1. Char the onion and ginger: Place the halved onions and sliced ginger cut-side down into a dry, heavy skillet over high heat. Sear them until they are well-charred, then flip and repeat before setting them aside.
  2. Toast the spices: In a dry pan over medium-high heat, lightly toast the star anise, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, and coriander seeds for about 3 minutes until fragrant.

2. Clean the Beef:

  1. Initial Blanch: Place the brisket and bones in a large pot and cover with water. Bring to a boil for 5 minutes to release impurities.
  2. Rinse: Drain the pot and thoroughly wash the icky residue off each bone and the brisket under cold running water.

3. Simmer the Broth:

  1. Start the base: Clean the pot, refill with 3.5 liters (3.75 quarts) of fresh water, and bring to a boil.
  2. Combine: Add the cleaned bones and brisket back in, followed by the charred onion and ginger, toasted spices, sugar, and salt. Ensure the water just barely covers the ingredients.
  3. Long Simmer: Cover with a lid and let it simmer gently for 3 hours.
  4. Finish the liquid: Remove the tender brisket and set it aside to cool, then refrigerate for later. Continue simmering the remaining broth, uncovered, for another 40 minutes.
  5. Refine: Strain the liquid into a clean pot, discarding the solids. You should have roughly 2.5 liters (2.65 quarts) of broth. If you have much more, simmer it down to concentrate the flavor.
  6. Final Seasoning: Stir in the fish sauce and do a final taste test, adding a pinch more salt or sugar if needed to reach a balanced, savory-sweet profile.

4. Assemble the Bowls:

  1. Noodles: Cook your rice sticks according to the package instructions right before you are ready to eat.
  2. Layer: Divid the noodles among the bowls. Top with a few slices of the cooked brisket and the very thinly sliced raw beef.
